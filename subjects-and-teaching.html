Subjects & Teaching

We have approx. 5-6 teams pr. year with an average of 6 young people in each team. The teams are mixed boys / girls. Each team has a common room, but teaching will also take place in other premises. The STU is 840 hours a year distributed over 40 weeks. Meeting time is Monday to Thursday at. 9.00-14.40 (2 modules before lunch and 2 modules after lunch) and Friday at. 9.00-12.30 (2 modules) - breaks are included in the form. Some subjects you must have (compulsory subjects), while other subjects you can choose from (electives). We try to create all electives from year to year, but it depends on how many people choose it.

Mandatory Subjects

English is an important language as it appears in every possible context in everyday life. With us, the English language is used in all sorts of ways; we read different kinds of texts, listen to readings and others' answers, ask questions and answer, present, write small tasks, watch movies / programs - and play and quizzes.

We work with many different topics throughout the year - and also like to start with your / your wishes and interests.

Electives

You get the opportunity to practice preparing different types of dishes, eg.

  • appetizers
  • snacking
  • main meals
  • desserts and baking
  • dishes you want to try yourself

In addition, you learn:

  • how to use a recipe
  • to experiment
  • about practical kitchen hygiene
  • Make it easiest for yourself in the kitchen
  • to make things fit together so the food is finished at the same time
  • tricks that make cooking easier

The way you learn it is by:

  • to practice concrete techniques (eg how to peel root vegetables)
  • to use electric aids (hand mixer, blender, microwave, stove etc.)
  • Always work with individual recipes to suit your experience

Here you get the opportunity to learn new and exciting board games or card games, while at the same time enhancing many different skills and skills. The teaching will focus on more complex games that generally reinforce learning, imagination, conscious planning and strategic thinking.

We play different games, eg Matador and other board games, various card games, and if you have some games yourself, you are welcome to bring them.

We practice and learn, but the primary is the social element.

In many games cards and rules are used in English, so if you master the English language reasonably, it is fine, but it is not a requirement.

Now you get the opportunity to learn or refresh your chess skills. Newcomer, practitioner or chess player - everyone is welcome.

We refresh from scratch:

  • placement of pieces and their movement
  • rules
  • how to open the game best
  • review of different openings
  • the importance of the center
  • the importance of the center game
  • Playoffs
  • checkmate

There will be opportunities to play against others and against computers. There can be played with and without time, and there will be the opportunity to undergo famous parties.

The goal is that you get a better feel for your body and mind. The teaching will consist of 2 parts: yoga and mindfulness / meditation.

Yoga is the practice of breathing technique and body postures, where you get all your muscles trained and make your body work. The breathing techniques help to create calmness in the body and strengthen concentration / attention.

Mindfulness / meditation is the training of the mind. Through the meditation you learn to train full attention on thoughts, feelings, body sensations and surroundings. You train in becoming more present, stopping automatic negative lines of thought / thought patterns and gaining control of thoughts.

Yoga and mindfulness contain many useful techniques that can help achieve more peace and understanding of your body and its signals.

You don't have to have tried either yoga or mindfulness before. It all takes place at a pace where everyone can join. The only thing that is required is that you are willing to try something new and you are ready for a subject where there is not much talk and where there is room for calm and contemplation.

We go through a PC from scratch and up. We look at what parts it is built on, how they work and what they are used for. In addition to the hardware of the machine, we look at the software, starting with the operating system and from there to various utilities needed in a modern PC.

It is important to help each other, we focus on sharing knowledge. We do this by mixing those who know a lot with those who do not.

You will be able to meet one or more of the following tasks during the year:

  • We look at prices and products in relation to buying or building a PC
  • As far as possible, we take everything that is connected to the machine (eg network etc.)
  • We look at information search on the internet
  • What are the possibilities of free software?
  • Security in relation to malware, virus and hacking
  • Installing a computer (Windows) from scratch
  • Configuring a wireless network
  • Continuous maintenance of the school's net café
  • You will try to make a network cable

The team will also be challenged with external tasks to the extent possible. We will do the tasks in practice, and it can be tasks such as:

  • Troubleshoot troubled machines
  • Offer on building new machines
  • Setting up machines
  • Offers for upgrading machines
